Course Overview

Biotechnology is an interdisciplinary field that combines biological sciences with engineering and technology to manipulate living organisms, cells, and biological systems for various applications. The course typically covers fundamental concepts such as genetics, molecular biology, and biochemistry. Students learn about the structure and function of nucleic acids and proteins, gene expression and regulation, and genetic engineering techniques, including CRISPR and recombinant DNA technology.

Syllabus

  • Week 2: Molecular Biology
  • Week 3: Genetic Engineering
  • Week 4: Microbiology
